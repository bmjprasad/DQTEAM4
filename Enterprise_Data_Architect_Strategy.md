# Enterprise Data Architect Function Strategy
## For Data & Analytics Organization - Senior Leadership Presentation

---

## Executive Summary

The Enterprise Data Architect (EDA) function is a critical strategic role that will establish the foundational data architecture, governance framework, and technology standards necessary to transform our organization into a data-driven enterprise. This role will bridge the gap between business strategy and technical implementation, ensuring our data assets become a competitive advantage.

---

## Strategic Objectives

### 1. **Data Strategy Alignment**
- Align data architecture with business objectives and digital transformation goals
- Establish enterprise-wide data standards and governance framework
- Create a unified data strategy that supports all business units

### 2. **Technology Modernization**
- Modernize legacy data systems and infrastructure
- Implement cloud-first, scalable data architecture
- Establish data platform strategy for analytics, AI/ML, and real-time processing

### 3. **Data Governance & Quality**
- Implement enterprise data governance framework
- Establish data quality standards and monitoring
- Ensure regulatory compliance (GDPR, CCPA, SOX, etc.)

### 4. **Business Enablement**
- Enable self-service analytics and data democratization
- Accelerate time-to-insight for business stakeholders
- Support advanced analytics and AI/ML initiatives

### 5. **Risk Management**
- Mitigate data security and privacy risks
- Establish disaster recovery and business continuity plans
- Ensure data lineage and auditability

---

## Key Focus Areas

### 1. **Enterprise Data Architecture**
- **Data Modeling**: Design logical and physical data models
- **Data Integration**: Establish ETL/ELT processes and data pipelines
- **Data Storage**: Define data lake, data warehouse, and data mart strategies
- **API Management**: Design data APIs and microservices architecture

### 2. **Data Governance & Stewardship**
- **Data Catalog**: Implement enterprise data catalog and metadata management
- **Data Lineage**: Track data flow and transformations
- **Data Quality**: Establish quality metrics and monitoring
- **Master Data Management**: Define master data strategies

### 3. **Technology & Platform Strategy**
- **Cloud Architecture**: Design multi-cloud and hybrid cloud strategies
- **Data Platforms**: Select and implement modern data platforms
- **Security Architecture**: Design data security and access controls
- **Performance Optimization**: Ensure scalability and performance

### 4. **Standards & Best Practices**
- **Data Standards**: Define naming conventions, data types, and formats
- **Architecture Patterns**: Establish reusable architecture patterns
- **Development Standards**: Create data engineering best practices
- **Documentation**: Maintain comprehensive architecture documentation

### 5. **Business Partnership**
- **Stakeholder Engagement**: Partner with business leaders and IT teams
- **Change Management**: Drive organizational change for data initiatives
- **Training & Enablement**: Educate teams on data architecture principles
- **Vendor Management**: Evaluate and manage data technology vendors

---

## Organizational Structure

### Reporting Structure
- **Reports to**: Chief Data Officer (CDO) or Chief Technology Officer (CTO)
- **Matrix reporting**: Works closely with business unit leaders
- **Team size**: 3-5 direct reports (Senior Data Architects, Data Engineers)

### Key Relationships
- **Business Stakeholders**: C-suite, business unit heads, product managers
- **IT Leadership**: CTO, IT Directors, Security team
- **Data Teams**: Data Scientists, Analytics teams, Data Engineers
- **External Partners**: Cloud providers, technology vendors, consultants

- **Hire Enterprise Data Architect**

### Phase 2: Build (Months 4-9)

- **Deploy data governance tools** and processes

### Phase 3: Scale (Months 10-18)

- **Optimize performance** and costs

### Phase 4: Innovate (Months 19+)
